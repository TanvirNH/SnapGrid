Frequently Asked Questions

Why does SnapGrid need Accessibility permission?

macOS requires explicit permission before any app may move or resize another app's windows. SnapGrid uses the system Accessibility API solely to position windows when you ask it to — nothing else. Grant it in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security ▸ Accessibility.

My shortcuts stopped working. What do I do?

Open System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security ▸ Accessibility and check that SnapGrid is listed and switched on. If it is already on, toggle it off and on once. This can be needed after an app update.

How do I snap a window?

  • Keyboard: e.g. ⌃⌥← for the left half, ⌃⌥↩ to maximize. All shortcuts are customizable in Settings.
  • Drag: drag a window to a screen edge or corner and release when the preview appears.
  • Palette: hold ⌥ while dragging to pick a snap target from the floating palette.

Can I exclude an app from window management?

Yes — Settings ▸ Behavior ▸ Excluded Apps.

Does SnapGrid work with multiple displays?

Yes. ⌃⌥⌘→ and ⌃⌥⌘← move the focused window between displays, keeping its proportions.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 12, 2026

The short version

SnapGrid collects no data. Period.

Data collection

SnapGrid does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information or usage data. There are no analytics, no trackers, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party SDKs. The app makes no network connections of its own.

What stays on your Mac

Your settings (shortcuts, preferences, excluded apps) and saved window layouts are stored locally on your Mac and never leave it.

Accessibility permission

SnapGrid asks for macOS Accessibility permission only so it can move and resize windows at your request. This permission is never used to read your screen content, log keystrokes, or observe your activity.

Email

If you choose to contact support or send feedback, your email is used solely to respond to you and is never shared with anyone.
